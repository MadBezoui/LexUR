\title{\textbf{Lexicographic Minimax Regret over Declared Monotone Probes\\
for Robust Multicriteria Selection}}
\author{Madani Bezoui}
\address{CESI LINEACT, UR 7527}
\begin{abstract}
The efficient set is a safety filter, not a complete decision rule. To provide a principled recommendation when the decision maker cannot state precise weights, we propose the Leximax Universal-Regret (LexUR) core. LexUR evaluates alternatives over a declared family of monotone questions (probes). It ranks candidates by the lexicographically sorted vector of their dimensionless regrets, returning a robust stability class accompanied by an interpretable certificate listing the binding questions.

We prove existence and completeness, Pareto compatibility, tolerance-rule stability under bounded perturbations, and a probe perturbation bound. We also provide an adaptive correlation-clustering construction that reduces sensitivity to redundant criteria.

In a comprehensive audit protocol spanning 11 methods, 10 preference families, and 8 front geometries, LexUR is competitive with the strongest robust scalarisation baselines on tail held-out loss. It remains non-inferior to ASF and sampled minimax regret under paired tests, while a single-point hypervolume score obtains the best average rank. LexUR's distinct contributions are its auditable regret certificate and reduced redundancy bias relative to averaging methods. Because point-estimate normalisation can be sensitive, robust deployment should report an interval stability class. A unique recommendation is returned only when margin conditions hold. LexUR is robust with respect to the declared probe family, not all possible preferences. We also outline an exploratory stochastic extension illustrated on a synthetic smart-grid economic dispatch model.
\end{abstract}
