\section{Smart-grid economic dispatch formulation}
\label{app:smartgrid}
The case study in \S\ref{sec:exp} formulates a 24-hour economic dispatch on a 14-generator system. The decision variables are the scheduled power $p_{gt}$ and commitment status $u_{gt}$ for generator $g$ at hour $t$. The uncertain parameters are the demand $d_t$, non-dispatchable renewable generation $w_t(\omega)$ (wind) and $s_t(\omega)$ (solar) under scenario $\omega$, and line losses $\ell_t(\omega)$.

The power-balance constraint ensures that total generation meets demand and losses:
\begin{equation}
\sum_g p_{gt} + w_t(\omega) + s_t(\omega) = d_t + \ell_t(\omega), \quad \forall t, \omega.
\end{equation}
Generators are subject to minimum and maximum capacity bounds and ramping constraints $R_g$:
\begin{equation}
P_g^{\min}u_{gt} \le p_{gt} \le P_g^{\max}u_{gt}, \qquad |p_{g,t}-p_{g,t-1}| \le R_g.
\end{equation}
The six objectives to be minimised are:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Cost:} Expected total fuel cost.
    \item \textbf{Emissions:} Total $\mathrm{CO}_2$ emissions.
    \item \textbf{Unreliability:} Loss of load probability.
    \item \textbf{Ramping wear:} Total absolute generation change $\sum_{g,t} |p_{g,t}-p_{g,t-1}|$.
    \item \textbf{Non-renewable fraction:} Ratio of thermal to total generation.
    \item \textbf{Peak gap:} Difference between the maximum and average net demand.
\end{enumerate}

The model is evaluated using sample-average approximation over 100 scenarios. All parameter values are available in the open-source repository.
