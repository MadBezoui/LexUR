\section{Smart-grid economic dispatch formulation}
\label{app:smartgrid}
The case study in \S\ref{sec:exp} formulates a 24-hour stochastic economic dispatch with recourse on a 14-generator test case (derived from the IEEE 14-bus structure). The first-stage decision variables are the day-ahead unit commitment status $u_{gt}$. The second-stage (recourse) variables under scenario $\omega$ are the scheduled power $p_{gt}(\omega)$, renewable curtailment $c_t^{\mathrm{ren}}(\omega)$, and load shedding $s_t^{\mathrm{shed}}(\omega)$. The uncertain parameters are the demand $d_t(\omega)$, non-dispatchable renewable generation $w_t(\omega)$ (wind) and $s_t(\omega)$ (solar) under scenario $\omega$, and line losses $\ell_t(\omega)$.

The power-balance constraint ensures that total generation meets demand and losses:
\begin{equation}
\sum_g p_{gt}(\omega) + w_t(\omega) + s_t(\omega) - c_t^{\mathrm{ren}}(\omega) + s_t^{\mathrm{shed}}(\omega) = d_t(\omega) + \ell_t(\omega), \quad \forall t, \omega.
\end{equation}
Generators are subject to minimum and maximum capacity bounds and ramping constraints $R_g$:
\begin{equation}
P_g^{\min}u_{gt} \le p_{gt}(\omega) \le P_g^{\max}u_{gt}, \qquad |p_{g,t}(\omega)-p_{g,t-1}(\omega)| \le R_g, \quad \forall \omega.
\end{equation}
The six objectives to be minimised are:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Cost:} Expected total fuel cost.
    \item \textbf{Emissions:} Expected total $\mathrm{CO}_2$ emissions.
    \item \textbf{Unreliability:} Loss of load probability $f_3(x)=\mathbb{P}\left(\sum_t s_t^{\mathrm{shed}}(\omega)>0\right)$.
    \item \textbf{Ramping wear:} Expected absolute generation change $\mathbb{E}[\sum_{g,t} |p_{g,t}(\omega)-p_{g,t-1}(\omega)|]$.
    \item \textbf{Non-renewable fraction:} Expected ratio of thermal to total generation.
    \item \textbf{Peak stress:} Expected difference between the maximum and average dispatch-dependent net load on the thermal fleet.
\end{enumerate}

The model is evaluated using a Monte-Carlo sample-average approximation over 100 scenarios. All parameter values (generator data, costs, emissions, ramp limits, and scenario bounds) are available in the open-source repository. This formulation serves as an illustrative application of the robust certificate framework.
