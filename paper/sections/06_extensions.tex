\section{Extensions: stochastic and multi-stakeholder decision making}
\label{sec:ext}

\subsection{Stochastic regret certificates}
When criteria are noisy, model $f_i(x)\sim P_i(x)$. Each probe value is then a
random variable; for non-linear probes (e.g.\ $q_{\max}$) its mean $\mu_q(x)$ and
standard deviation $\sigma_q(x)$ are \emph{not} simple functions of the criterion
means and variances and---because criteria may be correlated---we estimate them
by Monte-Carlo from joint samples rather than from marginal moments. The
normalisation anchors $q^\star,q^{\mathrm w}$ are taken on the estimated means. We
define the \emph{$\alpha$-confidence regret}
\begin{equation}
\reg_q^\alpha(x)=\frac{\mu_q(x)+z_\alpha\,\sigma_q(x)-q^\star}{q^{\mathrm w}-
q^\star+\varepsilon},
\end{equation}
with $z_\alpha$ the standard-normal quantile, penalising both expected
disappointment and its uncertainty, and apply the tolerance-based leximax order
to $(\reg_{q_1}^\alpha,\dots,\reg_{q_K}^\alpha)$. We present this as a practical
risk-adjusted rule; the asymptotic statement below uses the plug-in case
$\alpha{=}\tfrac12$ and the tolerance rule of \S\ref{sec:core}.

\begin{proposition}[Plug-in consistency]
\label{thm:stoch}
Let probe means be estimated from $n$ i.i.d.\ joint observations, with Lipschitz
probes and finite second moments, and use the $\tau$-tolerance leximax order with
$\tau>0$ fixed. Under the margin condition of Theorem~\ref{thm:stab}, the plug-in
stochastic-LUR winner equals the true LUR winner with probability tending to one
as $n\to\infty$.
\end{proposition}
\S\ref{sec:exp} gives an empirical confirmation: the recovery rate of the true
winner rises monotonically with the number of observations.

\subsection{Multi-stakeholder (Rawlsian) variant}
Let $\mathcal S=\{1,\dots,S\}$ index stakeholders, each with importance weights
$w_s\in\Delta^m$ inducing a stakeholder-specific disappointment $\reg_q^s(x)$.
The \emph{Rawlsian LUR} applies the leximax order to the worst-stakeholder
disappointments,
\begin{equation}
x^{\mathrm{LUR\text{-}R}}\in\argmin_{x}{}^{\mathrm{lex}}\;
\mathrm{sort}^{\downarrow}\!\Big(\max_{s}\reg_{q_1}^s(x),\dots,
\max_{s}\reg_{q_K}^s(x)\Big),
\end{equation}
optionally with a proportional-fairness cap $\reg_q^s(x)\le(1+\beta)\rho_1$ for
all $s,q$. It satisfies \emph{anonymity} (invariance to relabelling
stakeholders), \emph{monotonicity} (it does not worsen if every stakeholder's
regret falls), and \emph{worst-stakeholder protection} (no stakeholder exceeds
$(1+\beta)$ times the minimum worst regret). We claim these properties, not
uniqueness or independence of irrelevant alternatives.
