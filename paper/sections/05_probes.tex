\section{Adaptive, redundancy-aware probe generation}
\label{sec:probes}

\subsection{The redundancy problem and correlation clustering}
Real criteria are often redundant, tracking the same underlying concern. Averaging selection rules implicitly over-weight such a group by counting it once per member. We propose an adaptive probe family that reduces this bias by clustering empirically redundant criteria. 

We group redundant criteria by single-linkage connected components of the graph whose edges join pairs with Pearson correlation $\Corr(f_i,f_j)\ge\theta$. Redundancy is positive correlation: two criteria that rise and fall together carry duplicate information, whereas negatively correlated criteria represent genuine trade-offs and must remain separate. This yields clusters $C_1,\dots,C_c$.

\subsection{The adaptive probe family}
LexUR always includes the $m$ singletons (required for separation), the grand mean, and the grand maximum. It then adds one mean and one maximum probe \emph{per cluster} of size $>1$:
\begin{equation}
\Q=\{q_i\}_{i=1}^m\;\cup\;\{q_\mu,q_{\max}\}\;\cup\;
\bigcup_{|C_k|>1}\{q_{C_k,\mathrm{mean}},\,q_{C_k,\max}\}.
\end{equation}
Figure~\ref{fig:probes} illustrates the construction. A redundant group contributes one aggregate question rather than a separate aggregate per member. This reduces, but does not eliminate, redundancy bias because the individual singletons remain.

\begin{theorem}[Pareto compatibility for adaptive probes]
\label{thm:nonneg}
If every probe is monotone, LexUR is weakly Pareto compatible. If the family contains the singleton probes, LexUR is strictly Pareto compatible.
\end{theorem}

\subsection{Perturbation bound}
The reduction effect can be quantified as a regret-value perturbation lemma. This bounds the change in individual disappointments, not the change in the LexUR ranking.

\begin{lemma}[Disappointment perturbation]
\label{thm:approx}
Suppose for a full-family probe $q$ there is a reduced-family probe $q'$ with $\sup_r|q(r)-q'(r)|\le\eta$, and normalisation ranges satisfy $q^{\mathrm w}-q^\star,\,q'^{\mathrm w}-q'^\star\ge\beta>0$. Then for every $x$:
\[
|\reg_q(x)-\reg_{q'}(x)|\;\le\;\frac{4\eta}{\beta}.
\]
\end{lemma}
The explicit proof is provided in the appendix. The bound is informative only when the probe's attainable range is bounded away from zero. Ultimately, correlation clustering is an empirical heuristic whose efficacy is demonstrated in \S\ref{sec:exp}.

\subsection{Empirical reduction}
Table~\ref{tab:reduction} and Figure~\ref{fig:reduction} report the realised reduction. The full coalition family has size $2(2^m-1)-m$. The adaptive family is linear in $m$, achieving a nearly four-thousand-fold reduction by $m=15$ while preserving decision quality.
